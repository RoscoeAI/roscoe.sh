# Roscoe Frontier Brand Kit

## Visual Thesis
Roscoe should feel like a frontier dispatch office at dusk: parchment notices pinned to plank walls, telegraph brass catching the last campfire light, and a strange cheerful sense that the next wagon west is actually a fleet of LLM workers.

## Content Plan
- Hero: brand, promise, opt-in CTA, atmospheric frontier tableau
- Consent proof: clean legal-grade signup block with explicit SMS consent
- Product story: how Roscoe and the Guild work together
- Trust/docs close: policy links, proof-of-consent references, operational credibility

## Interaction Thesis
- Slow wagon-wheel orbit or brass seal rotation in the hero
- Telegraph ticker blink or signal pulse across section dividers
- Paper-reveal section transitions with slight vertical drift and dust-glow fades

## Brand Keywords
- frontier dispatch
- telegraph office
- campfire operator console
- handbill poster
- brass, parchment, cinder, ink
- weirdly trustworthy
- adventurous but precise

## Typography
- Headline font: `Rye`
- Body/UI font: `Spectral`
- Fallback serif stack:
  - `"Spectral", "Iowan Old Style", "Palatino Linotype", "Book Antiqua", Georgia, serif`

- River: `#2A4D63`
- Ember: `#9C4E2E`

## Supporting Tones
- Dust line: `#C8AE7D`
- Lantern haze: `rgba(255, 214, 145, 0.18)`
- Deep night: `#140F0B`
- Ledger gray: `#5F5144`

## Surface Rules
- The hero should read like a poster, not an app shell.
- Use sections, rules, hanging ornaments, stamps, and split editorial layout before using cards.
- Compliance areas can become cleaner and calmer than the rest of the page, but they should still feel native to the Roscoe world.
- Avoid generic gradients, glossy SaaS shadows, dashboard cards, and tech-blue defaults.

## Motion Rules
- Motion should feel mechanical or atmospheric, not digital.
- Prefer subtle repeated motion over fast looping gimmicks.
- If a motion cannot be imagined existing in a physical telegraph office, cut it.

## Copy Voice
- Clear, steady, frontier-operator language
- A little theatrical, never jokey in compliance surfaces
- Product copy can be colorful; consent copy must be plain

## Hero Copy Direction
- Brand first: `ROSCOE`
- Promise second: autopilot for Claude and Codex
- Support line: Roscoe watches the wire while Guild workers push the build west

## Twilio-Facing UX Notes
- Consent form should look trustworthy before it looks clever.
- Checkbox must be explicit and unchecked by default.
- Terms and privacy links should be visible directly beside or beneath the consent action.
- Help/STOP/rates language should be visible on the consent page, not buried.
